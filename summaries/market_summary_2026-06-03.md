## AI Infrastructure Rally Drives Tech Leadership

A major theme emerged around AI infrastructure, propelling semiconductor and data center stocks sharply higher. **Marvell Technology (MRVL)** surged 32.52% after Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang publicly called it a potential "next trillion-dollar company," catalyzing enthusiasm for the AI hardware supply chain. **Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE)** jumped 19.47%, while **Aehr Test Systems (AEHR)** gained 20.70% and **Penguin Solutions (PENG)** rose 18.32%—all benefiting from the AI infrastructure buildout narrative.

Nvidia itself announced expanded AI platform offerings including new CPUs and PC products, though its shares dipped 0.69% in a modest profit-taking move. Apple climbed 2.90%, while Tesla added 1.89%, but other mega-cap tech faced headwinds: Microsoft plunged 4.17%, Alphabet fell 3.86%, and Amazon declined 1.81%, suggesting sector rotation within technology.

## Market Concentration Concerns and Fed Backdrop

MarketWatch noted a "breadth paradox" warning despite the S&P 500's rally, highlighting narrowing leadership and elevated valuations now surpassing 1929 levels. Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon characterized markets as being in "greed mode" as AI companies pursue massive capital raises. This concentration risk looms as the Federal Reserve released minutes from recent FOMC meetings, though no explicit policy shifts were announced in the limited headlines.

## Cross-Asset Signals

Bitcoin gained 0.38% to $66,959, though reports noted long-time crypto bulls "rage quitting" their positions. The dollar strengthened modestly against the yen (USD/JPY +0.21%) while weakening slightly against the euro and pound.

**Legend Biotech (LEGN)** topped gainers with a 42.22% surge, reflecting biotech volatility.
