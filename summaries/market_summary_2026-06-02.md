# Daily Market Summary — June 02, 2026

## Market Overview

U.S. equities finished modestly higher on Tuesday, with the S&P 500 gaining 0.26% and the Nasdaq 100 advancing 0.60%, though headline moves masked significant dispersion beneath the surface. The Dow Jones added just 0.09%, while small-caps lagged with the Russell 2000 dropping 0.47%. The VIX ticked up slightly to 16.15, reflecting mild nervousness despite the positive close.

## Tech Sector Divergence and AI Infrastructure Boom

Mega-cap tech delivered sharply mixed results. Nvidia surged 6.26% after CEO Jensen Huang endorsed Marvell Technology as "the next trillion-dollar company" at Computex, sparking a 16%+ rally in Marvell shares overnight. Microsoft climbed 2.28%, benefiting from continued AI optimism. However, the session's biggest story was Alphabet's announcement of an $80 billion equity raise to fund AI infrastructure buildout—shares fell 1.04% as investors digested the dilution implications despite the strategic rationale.

Elsewhere in mega-cap land, selling pressure intensified: Meta plunged 5.07%, Tesla dropped 4.57%, and Amazon declined 3.47%. Apple slipped 1.84%. The bifurcation underscores mounting investor selectivity around AI capex strategies and valuation discipline, even as enthusiasm for semiconductor and infrastructure plays remains elevated.

## Commodities and Geopolitical Signals

Gold rallied strongly, gaining 1.79% to $4,555, signaling safe-haven demand amid rising geopolitical tensions. Reports surfaced that Iran halted U.S. negotiations and threatened to "completely block" the Strait of Hormuz, raising energy security concerns. Despite this, crude oil fell 1.61% to $90.68 after President Trump attempted to reassure markets that a peace deal is forthcoming. The divergence between gold strength and oil weakness suggests traders are hedging tail risks while betting on near-term diplomatic progress.

Bitcoin dropped 3.00% to $69,183, extending its recent pullback as risk appetite cooled.

## Cross-Asset Themes

The dollar firmed modestly, with USD/JPY rising to 159.75, while EUR/USD and GBP/USD were essentially flat. The combination of a stronger dollar, higher gold, and weakness in small-caps and risk assets like crypto points to a cautious macro backdrop. Federal Reserve minutes from April meetings were released but appeared to have limited market impact, suggesting no major policy surprises.

The session highlighted stark sector rotation: AI infrastructure winners continue to outperform, while questions around capital intensity and dilution weigh on others.
